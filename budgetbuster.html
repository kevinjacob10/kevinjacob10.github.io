Budget Buster

Gameplay & Systems Programmer

Project Overview

Budget Buster is an educational grocery-shopping simulation designed to teach food literacy and budgeting skills to single-parent families. Players manage a limited budget while selecting culturally relevant and nutritionally balanced food items, learning to differentiate between needs and wants while tracking real-time spending.

The game emphasizes sustainable decision-making by incorporating nutrition systems, shopping list management, budget tracking and realistic grocery aisle organization â€” all delivered through an accessible multi-platform experience.

  • Developed core budgeting system tracking cart totals against allocated budget with real-time feedback
  • Built dynamic shopping list system allowing item categorization (Needs vs Wants) and user-driven prioritization

Core Systems

Budget Tracking System

Real-time cart value computation subtracting purchases from allocated budget with immediate visual feedback and checkout validation logic.

Nutrition & Product Data System

Structured product database storing nutritional values, pricing, categorization, enabling informed player decisions and health-based evaluations.

Categorization & Shopping List

Interactive list management system allowing players to classify items as Needs or Wants and track priority-driven purchasing decisions.
